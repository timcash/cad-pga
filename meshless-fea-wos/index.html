Meshless FEA: PGA + SDF + Walk on Spheres

This example treats the hot and cold walls as exact PGA lines, the inner obstacle as a signed-distance constraint, and the field solve as a Walk on Spheres Monte Carlo process.

Distance kernel

For the polygonal walls, the boundary distance is read algebraically from |P ^ L|. For the center disk, it comes from the circle SDF sqrt(x^2 + y^2) - r.

Walk step

The pink walker queries the nearest boundary, draws the largest empty circle, then jumps to a random point on that circle. Repeating that process estimates the harmonic temperature field without a volume mesh.
