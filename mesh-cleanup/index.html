Simplicial Extension and Area

In Plane-Based Geometric Algebra (PGA), the magnitude of a k-complex can be evaluated by joining its boundary with an arbitrary gauge point o.

For a polygon, its area A is exactly half the ideal norm of the sum of the boundary edges E_i joined to o:

A = 0.5 * || o v (sum E_i) ||

Sum of Abs Cone Area: 0.000

Sum of the absolute areas of the 3D triangles. Changes as you move the apex.

Net Signed Area Norm: 0.000

Invariant. The true 2D area of the base polygon.
