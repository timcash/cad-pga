Why the net area stays constant while the cone changes.

The live demo triangulates a polygon boundary against a movable gauge point. The individual cone facets get steeper or flatter as the apex moves, but the signed net area encoded by the full boundary remains fixed.

Boundary
$$ \partial P = \sum_{i=1}^{n} E_i, \qquad E_i = p_i \vee p_{i+1} $$

The polygon is represented by its oriented boundary edges. In the ganja.js code, the join is written with & because we are working in dual 3D PGA.

Gauge Join
$$ \Pi(o) = o \vee \partial P $$

Joining the boundary with a gauge point produces a plane-like object whose ideal part retains the planar area information while the Euclidean height contributions cancel in the sum.

Invariant Area
$$ A(P) = \frac{1}{2}\left\| o \vee \sum_i E_i \right\|_{\infty} $$

This is the stable green value in the demo. The ideal norm extracts the true signed area of the base polygon, not the changing 3D cone area.

What The Red Number Means

The cone facets are not the invariant quantity

$$ A_{\text{abs}}(o) = \frac{1}{2} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left\| o \vee E_i \right\| $$

This sum uses absolute Euclidean facet areas, so it grows or shrinks as the apex moves. That makes it a useful contrast to the boundary-based invariant but not a replacement for it.

What The Green Number Means

The boundary sum remembers orientation

Because the edges are oriented, the non-area height terms cancel when you add them before joining with the gauge point. What survives is the net signed content of the original polygon.

Moving the apex changes the decomposition. It does not change the magnitude encoded by the oriented boundary.

Code Mapping

How the formulas map into the demo

  • edge = p1 & p2 builds each oriented boundary edge.
  • sumEdges is the running total for \( \sum_i E_i \).
  • netAreaPlane = apex & sumEdges computes \( o \vee \partial P \).
  • 0.5 * netAreaPlane.Length is the displayed net area scalar.
Geometric Takeaway

Why this matters for cleanup problems

Boundary-driven formulas are attractive in CAD because they can evaluate shape content without first forcing a fragile triangulation or picking one preferred fan decomposition.

That makes the example a good foundation for future mesh, polygon, and topological editing demos in this library.
