Look, Ma, No Matrices!

A carrier frame and a child sensor frame move as PGA motors.

Inspired by Steven De Keninck's matrix-free renderer write-up, this page keeps the idea deliberately small: a body frame, a sensor frustum, and three carried axes are all moved by composed motors instead of a `4x4` transform stack.

Frame stack

\[ \mathbf{M}_{\mathrm{sensor}}(t)= \mathbf{M}_{\mathrm{carrier}}(t)\,\mathbf{M}_{\mathrm{local}}(t), \qquad p' = \mathbf{M}\,p\,\widetilde{\mathbf{M}} \]

The faint frame at the origin is the local authoring space. The moving carrier and attached sensor are obtained by motor composition, and the colored axis tips show the same frame-transport idea that the article later optimizes for renderer basis directions.
