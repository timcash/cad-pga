Gear Hierarchy

Parent motion plus local motion gives one world transform.

This compact scene-graph example came over from the ga_cad/src_v1 plugin. The important idea is not the gear itself, but the way the arm inherits a parent transform and adds a local one.

Math Notes A clean walk through parent-local-world composition. Motor Primer A useful intuition builder for rigid motions and composed transforms. PGA Reference Projective Geometric Algebra for Computer Science.

Hierarchy equation

\[ M_{\mathrm{world}}(t) = M_{\mathrm{gear}}(t)\,M_{\mathrm{local}}(t), \qquad P_{\mathrm{new}} = M\,P\,\widetilde{M} \]

The arm is never authored in global coordinates. It is evaluated in the gear frame and then pushed out into world space.
